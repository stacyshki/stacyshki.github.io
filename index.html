Problem outline

The Earth is sending us signals, and these signals are hidden within the changes in global temperatures. Since the Industrial Revolution, the massive release of greenhouse gases from human activities has gradually altered the Earth's climate system, leading to a continuous rise in global temperatures and an increase in extreme weather events. These changes are not just fluctuations in numbers; they are profoundly impacting our lives, environment, and future.

Causes: According to NASA, Water Vapour, Carbon dioxide(CO₂), Methane, Nitrous oxide, and Sulfur hexafluoride contribute to the greenhouse effect. Over the last century, human activities have increased concentrations of this natural greenhouse.

Effects: Global climate change has already had observable effects on the environment. Glaciers have shrunk, ice on rivers and lakes is breaking up earlier, plant and animal ranges have shifted, and trees are flowering sooner. Effects that scientists had predicted in the past would result from global climate change: loss of sea ice accelerated sea-level rise and longer, more intense heat waves. Source: NASA

Conclusion

Through these charts, we see a vivid story about the Earth's changing temperatures. From 1961 to 2019, global temperatures have continued to rise, and seasonal temperature fluctuations have also quietly shifted. Temperature changes in certain months are particularly noticeable, and the frequency of extreme weather events seems to be increasing as well. These changes are not just numbers; they are part of our lives, affecting our environment, agriculture, and daily routines. Global warming is no longer a distant concept but a reality that each of us can feel. These charts remind us that the pace of climate change is accelerating, and we need to take action to address this challenge. Whether it's reducing carbon emissions or adapting to new climate realities, we all have a responsibility to contribute to the future of our planet. Let us draw strength from this data and work together to create a more sustainable and livable world for the next generation. The story of global warming continues, and each of us can play a positive role in it.
